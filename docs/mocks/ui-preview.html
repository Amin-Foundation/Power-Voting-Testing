The collaborative consensus engine

How it works

1. A survey creator (a GitHub-verified maintainer of the relevant repo) asks a question, seeding it with 10–15 suggested responses.

2. Participants sign in with a wallet, pick a role for that poll, and vote on responses. They can contribute their own additions; the survey prioritizes which ones to show in realtime.

3. Once at least 3 verified voters have participated, results are revealed publicly: top responses, key opinion groups, areas of consensus, and points of further exploration — broken out by stakeholder role.

Background

Power Voting is an extended version of Polis, an academically validated collective-response survey used with groups of 200,000+ by governments and independent media.

Polis is typically used as a large-scale opinion poll. Power Voting is for small communities that align around a shared mission.

How do you participate in Filecoin?

Pick the role you want to vote as today. We use it to group your vote alongside others like you in results — it does not change how much your vote counts. All polls are unweighted (1 verified person = 1 vote). You can pick a different role for the next poll. On the next step you'll sign a short message from your wallet (hot or cold) to prove the address is yours. No transaction is sent.

Cancel
What "verified" means: every voter gets one vote per poll, regardless of role. The wallet signature proves the address is yours and lets us dedupe voters across polls. Stakeholder roles additionally check role-specific conditions when you sign: Tokenholder requires ≥ 1 FIL on the signing address; Storage Provider requires the address to be a miner owner/worker; Client requires at least one active deal. Developer / Community is self-attested.
